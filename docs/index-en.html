What's that wave on the screen

An electron doesn't have an exact position in space — only a probability of being found at any given point. The purple curve is exactly that probability: the higher it is somewhere, the more likely you'd find the particle there. The orange bar is the barrier the particle is trying to cross.

Once the wave reaches the barrier, it doesn't have to stop entirely. Part of it passes through and keeps going on the other side — not because it found some hidden gap, but because at this scale the particle genuinely has a chance of ending up somewhere classical physics says it shouldn't.

This isn't just theory

The Sun shines because of this exact effect: atomic nuclei get close enough to fuse only by tunneling through the electrostatic repulsion between them. Flash memory in your phone works the same way. So does alpha decay in radioactive elements — the nucleus passes through a barrier that classical physics says should be impossible to cross.
